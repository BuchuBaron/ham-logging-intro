Intro to Electronic Logging

  1. Definition: A computer version of your paper station logbook.
  2. It could be ...
    • ... a computer program: spreadsheet, DXKeeper, Logger 32, SwissLog, XLog, (Google for more)
    • ... a website (LoTW, eQSL, ClugLog, etc.).
    • ... a combination of the above

Why You Should Keep Your Logs Online:

  1. Safer: your logs are kept and managed by professionals and are safer from computer crashes or theft
  2. More Functionality:
    • Easy for others to confirm QSOs
    • Sophisticated reporting tools
    • Automatic/Manual submissions for various awards
    • Look up details of a callsign
  3. Cheaper: no postage fees and hassle

LotW

Logbook of The World

http://www.arrl.org/logbook-of-the-world

    Logbook of the World (LoTW) is an online service that enables you to
  • electronically submit contacts (QSOs) for confirmation
  • view your submitted QSOs and resulting confirmations online
  • electronically submit confirmations for credit toward DXCC, VUCC, WAS, WAZ and WPX awards
  • view your DXCC, VUCC, WAS, WAZ and WPX award progress online

Electronic Logging Process

CTARC ILLW Logs

  1. During the ILLS weekend QSOs are logged on paper.
  2. The paper logs are captured electronically to be imported to various sites:
    1. LoTW,
    2. eQSL, and
    3. the SARL.

Setup TrustedQSL

  1. Uploads are done by a certificate-secured program called 'TrustedQSL'
  2. The first time, you need to request a certificate from the computer where it will be used. I got a new laptop and had to re-apply.
  3. It is possible to have more than one certificate/callsign/location, and club callsigns are supported.

SARL

Requirements for Capturing QSLs:

  1. The QSL account for CTARC needs to be linked to the profile of a paid up SARL member. You do this by emailing a request to the webmaster of the SARL site at webmaster@sarl.org.za.
  2. The site accepts only new ADIF format uploads, or CSV files with the following format: 
    • ZS0123,SSB,20050811,1200,40m
  3. The process is buggy and I ended up sending the CSV file to the webmaster by email to upload.
