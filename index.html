Intro to Electronic Logging

  1. Definition: A computer version of your paper station logbook.
  2. It could be ...
    • ... a computer program: spreadsheet, DXKeeper, Logger 32, SwissLog, XLog, (Google for more)
    • ... a website (LoTW, eQSL, ClugLog, etc.).
    • ... a combination of the above

Having your logs in a program on your PC in the shack is an improvement in functionality and efficiency, so why would you want to keep your logs online?

Why Online (Website) Logs:

1. Safer:

  • your logs are kept and managed by professionals and are safer from computer crashes or theft

2. More Functionality:

  • Easy for others to confirm QSOs
  • Automatic/Manual submissions for various awards
  • Look up details of a callsign

3. Cheaper:

  • no postage fees and hassle

Electronic Logging Process

CTARC ILLW Logs

  1. During the ILLW weekend QSOs are logged on paper.
  2. The paper logs are captured electronically.
  3. The electronic logs are imported to three sites:
    1. LoTW,
    2. eQSL, and
    3. the SARL.

2. Capturing the Logs

Paper logs (often hard to read or interpret)

Logging Program - XLog

An Open Source logging program. You can use any other program that allows exporting to ADIF and/or CSV.

Fire up XLog for a quick demo, especially of exporting logs.

Exporting from XLog

We need to export to:

  1. ADIF (for LoTW and eQSL), and
  2. CSV (for SARL).

3. Importing Log Data to QSL Websites

1. LoTW

Setup TrustedQSL

  1. Uploads are done by a certificate-secured program called 'TrustedQSL'
  2. The first time, you need to request a certificate from the computer where it will be used. When I got a new laptop I had to re-apply for a certificate (can be avoided if you take a backup of the configuration).
  3. You can have more than one certificate/callsign/location, and club callsigns are supported.

3. SARL

Requirements for Capturing QSLs:

  1. The QSL account for CTARC needs to be linked to the profile of a paid up SARL member. You do this by emailing a request to the webmaster of the SARL site at webmaster@sarl.org.za.
  2. The site accepts only new ADIF format uploads, or CSV files with the following format: 
    • ZS0123,SSB,20050811,1200,40m
  3. The process is buggy and I ended up sending the CSV file to the webmaster by email to upload.
